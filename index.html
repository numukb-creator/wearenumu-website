Ethics Gate™
AI Governance That's Built In — Not Bolted On

The UK's only proprietary ethical AI governance framework for SMEs. Built on Islamic principles of truthfulness, trustworthiness, and justice — and aligned with international standards.

What Is Ethics Gate™?

Ethics Gate™ is Numu's proprietary framework that governs every AI agent we deploy. It's not a checklist we run once and forget. It's a living governance layer that operates continuously inside your digital workforce.

Built on three Islamic ethical principles — Sidq (truthfulness), Amanah (trustworthiness), and 'Adl (justice) — Ethics Gate™ ensures that every AI output, every data access, and every automated decision meets verifiable ethical standards.

Only 10% of AI consultancies offer any ethical governance framework. We built ours into every workflow.

The Three Pillars

صِدق

Sidq — Truthfulness

Every AI output must be complete, contextualised, and verifiable.

✓ APPROVED

"Our Growth package typically delivers 12:1 ROI based on internal deployment data."

✗ REJECTED

"GUARANTEED 50x ROI in just 7 days!" — unverifiable, exaggerated claim

أمانة

Amanah — Trustworthiness

Client data, stakeholder dignity, and reputational integrity are protected at every step.

✓ APPROVED

Encrypted data transfer, role-based access, human override for sensitive operations.

✗ REJECTED

Sharing client data across accounts, sending without consent, scraping personal info.

عدل

'Adl — Justice

Fair treatment embedded in every workflow. No discriminatory patterns.

✓ APPROVED

Inclusive language, unbiased targeting, transparent pricing across all demographics.

✗ REJECTED

Excluding demographics from offers, differential pricing based on profile, biased copy.

From Principles to Production

Five implementation steps that turn ethical principles into operational governance.

1

Pre-deployment assessment

25-question diagnostic across 7 pillars before any agent goes live. Covers data handling, output accuracy, bias risk, privacy compliance, stakeholder impact, transparency, and override protocols.

2

Automated guardrails

Real-time bias detection, content screening, and compliance checks built into every agent workflow. Guardrails run continuously — not just at deployment.

3

Transparency logging

Every agent decision, data access, and output is logged for audit. Full traceability from input to output, with reasoning chains documented.

4

Human-in-the-loop triggers

Sensitive actions (publishing content, sending proposals, pricing decisions) require human approval via Slack/email. Escalation thresholds are configurable per client.

5

Quarterly re-certification

Ongoing governance review to ensure continued compliance. Updated scorecard, drift detection, and updated recommendations.

Decision Logic: Traffic Light System

Green — Approved

Output passes all three pillars. Deployed automatically. Logged for audit trail.

Yellow — Review

Potential concern flagged. Held for human review before deployment. Common for nuanced content or edge cases.

Red — Blocked

Output fails one or more pillars. Blocked from deployment. Flagged for remediation with specific violation details.

Applied to Every Deliverable

  • All AI-generated content passes Sidq (truthfulness) verification before publishing
  • All data handling complies with Amanah (trustworthiness) — encrypted, consented, minimal
  • All targeting and communications pass 'Adl (justice) bias checks
  • All automated decisions include transparency logging and audit trails
  • All sensitive actions include human-in-the-loop override triggers
  • All deployments include quarterly Ethics Gate™ re-certification

Challenge

Solo founder with 25 years of experience but zero team, building a consultancy from scratch while producing daily content, managing outreach, writing proposals, researching competitors, and handling all operations. The bottleneck was clear: one person can't scale.

Deployed an 18-agent multi-agent system internally using CrewAI + n8n orchestration, with Ethics Gate™ governance on every workflow. Privacy Policy

How we collect, use, and protect your data.

Last updated: March 2026

Numu Consulting ("we", "us", "our") is committed to protecting your privacy. This privacy policy explains how we collect, use, and safeguard your personal data in accordance with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018.

1. Data Controller

Numu Consulting is the data controller for the purposes of applicable data protection legislation.

Contact: salam@wearenumu.com

2. Information We Collect

  • Contact Information: Name, email address, phone number, business name provided through forms on our website
  • Business Information: Industry, team size, budget range, operational challenges shared during consultations
  • Usage Data: Pages visited, time spent, browser type, device information collected through analytics
  • Communication Data: Email correspondence, call notes, meeting records created during our engagement

3. How We Use Your Data

  • To provide our consulting services and deliver digital workforce solutions
  • To respond to enquiries and schedule discovery calls
  • To send relevant resources, insights, and newsletter content (only with your explicit consent)
  • To improve our website and services through analytics
  • To comply with legal obligations

4. Legal Basis for Processing

  • Consent: For marketing communications, newsletter subscriptions, and resource downloads
  • Legitimate Interest: For service delivery, website improvement, and business operations
  • Contractual Necessity: For processing related to service agreements and project delivery
  • Legal Obligation: For compliance with applicable laws and regulations

5. Data Sharing

We do not sell your personal data. We may share data with:

  • Service providers who assist in delivering our services (e.g., email platforms, CRM tools, scheduling software)
  • Professional advisors (legal, accounting) as required
  • Regulatory authorities when required by law

All third-party processors are bound by data processing agreements and must comply with UK GDPR.

6. Data Retention

We retain personal data only for as long as necessary for the purposes it was collected:

  • Enquiry data: 2 years from last contact
  • Client data: Duration of contract + 6 years (in line with HMRC requirements)
  • Marketing consent: Until withdrawn
  • Website analytics: 26 months

7. Your Rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the right to:

  • Access your personal data (Subject Access Request)
  • Rectify inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Erase your data ("right to be forgotten")
  • Restrict processing of your data
  • Data portability — receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format
  • Object to processing based on legitimate interest
  • Withdraw consent at any time without affecting prior processing

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at salam@wearenumu.com. We will respond within 30 days.

8. Cookies

We use essential cookies for website functionality and analytics cookies (with your consent) to understand how visitors use our site. You can manage cookie preferences through your browser settings.

Types of cookies we use:

  • Essential cookies: Required for the website to function properly (e.g., session management)
  • Analytics cookies: Help us understand how visitors interact with our website (e.g., Google Analytics)
  • Preference cookies: Remember your settings and preferences for a better experience

9. International Transfers

Where data is processed outside the UK (e.g., by cloud service providers), we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place, including Standard Contractual Clauses approved by the ICO or UK adequacy decisions.

10. Security

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your data, including encryption in transit and at rest, access controls, and regular security assessments. Our Ethics Gate™ framework provides additional governance for any AI-processed data.

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Any changes will be posted on this page with an updated revision date. We encourage you to review this page periodically.

12. Contact & Complaints

For privacy enquiries: salam@wearenumu.com

You have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO): ico.org.uk
